About

Learn More About Our AI Detection

We built an AI model that classifies a mushroom's genus. 🍄

The model works by taking in an image of a mushroom and it will shortly return its predicted genus.

3 simple steps:

  • Take or find a picture of a mushroom
  • Upload your image
  • Learn the genus of the mushroom

Why did we choose to make this model? With more people staying home for work, there is a growing need to be surrounded by nature. A popular way to get out of the house is to go out into nature and explore, where you may see numerous species of mushrooms. However, as mushroom encounters increase, misidentification by novice mushroom explorers have increased as well. This is where our Mushroom classification AI will be most useful in helping prevent most of these misidentifications.

Pipeline

Brainstorming

AUG 8 - 11 2022

While brainstorming ideas for our project, we came up with multiple different potential AI's to develop. Although, we ultimately landed on the consensus to code a mushroom identification program. Some other AI's were: a license plate detection program; a sign language translator; a flower identifier; a smile detection program; and a program that detects if you are driving correctly within the lanes.

Data Collection and Preparation

AUG 12 - 15 2022

We used Kaggle, a scientific logging website, to gather images of mushrooms within certain genera; and then used Roboflow to label and annotate the images. Finally, we got about 320 images for each class, 2800 images in total.

Model Training

AUG 16 - 18 2022

During the Model Training phase, we imported our results from datasets from Roboflow to begin training our AI. Using YOLOv5, we were able to train our data in epochs. We ran 250 epochs on our datasets and then had Weights & Biases print the metrics/results.

Model Evaluation and Iteration

AUG 18 - 19 2022

During our Model Evaluation phase, we used the metrics (Confusion Matrix, Loss Charts, and Confidence Curves) from YOLOv5 to first sort out any issues with our model. This included removing a class that was appearing as duplicated, etc. In addition, we also were able to test our model using images that we web scraped using SerpAPI.

Model Deployment

AUG 24 2022

During our Model Deployment phase, we were able to find a BootstrapMade template for our website and customize it to fit our project needs. We will also be implementing a method for users to upload their files and images to our website to use the Mushroom Classification AI.

Our Data

How did we collect and prepare our Data?

Kaggle

We used a prexisting Kaggle Dataset as our first and primary source of mushroom images to our AI.

Google SerpAPI

In addition to our Kaggle dataset, we supplemented it by using Google SerpAPI to web scrape additional images to evaluate our model's performance.

Preparation

In order to prepare our images for our AI's training, we uploaded our dataset to Roboflow, and assigned hundreds of images to each of our team members to annotate. To which, we would then upload to Google Colab where our model was located.

Roboflow

With Roboflow, our team was able to annotate our massive dataset, then Roboflow separated our images into a training, testing, and validation set. Finally, it resized and auto-oriented our images for training.

Modeling

YOLOv5

YOLOv5

YOLOv5, or "you only look once", is an object detection algorithm which divides an image into a grid system that will detect an object within itself.

  • Takes an image as input
  • Extracts region proposals
  • Computes CNN features
  • Classifies regions

Evaluation

Model Performance

Confusion Matrix

Confusion matrix: used to evaluate the performance of a classification model by comparing target vs. predicted values
The table to the above is the confusion matrix for our model. The left side of the table has the predicted values and the bottom side has the true values. Each of the dark blue boxes shows that the AI’s prediction matched its true value the majority of the time with only a few occasional errors.
There is a 90.67% accuracy.

Precision and Recall

Precision: measures the correct positive predictions.
Recall: measures the actual positive labels that were correctly classified in the model
The precision and recall graphs curve upwards, showing that the accuracy is increasing

Validation Loss

Validation loss: calculated error after running the validation set
Box_loss: measures how closely the model can put a bounding box over the object
Cls_loss: measures how accurately the model can split an image into smaller areas
The downward sloping graphs show that the loss is decreasing.

mAP

mAP: mean average precision
The graphs have upwards sloping curves, which shows that the model is becoming more stable and consistent.
